CUSTOM LOADER

Last week was a pretty short one because of the 12th so I didn't feel like I did enough to warrent a whole post for just that week. I dont think I am going to stick to having to do them each week anyway, I feel like the further I get into placement the bigger projects I will get and I don't want ot be writing thes eposts just for the sake of it, I want them to be useful to read back on.

To start of the week after the 12th I have been doing more emails, more builds that is, sometimes the designs are done by me and sometimes they are done by claire then I build them. I am also waiting on the Remus spec to come in so that I can have a go at doing one of those emails too.

I got something a bit different today as well, I was asked could I have a look at a custom loader for a app called smartcare, the loader isn't working on iOS. I don't know much about code so I didn't really know why it wasn't working but I see that the file is a lottie file and they are just animations made using after effects or any animation software so I thought I would just ask if they wanted me to try and make a custom loader instead of them using one they found online.

They said I could give it a go so I did, I created a heart animation on after effects. This was the first time in years I have used after effects, the last time was when I was in tech, it wasn't actaully as hard as I remember, I will put the lader down below.

FILE FORMAT

Once I finally got the loader down I then had a lot of trouble trying to get it in the right format for the client to be able to use it in there app. At the start I thought maybe they could use it if it was a gif but that wasn't the case, I needed it as a .json file. For me to get this I needed a plugin for after effects called "body moovin". Once I got my head around that I was able to export it in the format I needed.

That wasn't the end of the problems though. The next one was that the program they were using couldn't refernece images so I had to go back to after effects and find a way of doing it without images. Basically I was importing images I created on illustrator, instead what I should have been doing was creating the icon on illustrator and exporting it just as an illustrator file, then import that file into after effects and then going to "layer" and slected "create" and then "create shapes from vector layer". What that does is seperates each layer from the illustrator file into their own layers on after effects so that you can animate them individually.

Once I figured that out I was flying, and it was making me want to try and create more animations. I figured before this if I said that I could do it, it was going to go one of two ways, either I was able to do it and they would be happy with the outcome or I wouldn't be able to and they wouldn't be too bothered because they weren't thinking they would be able to create their own anyway. Thankfully it was the first option and it gave me a real sense of satisfaction becasue no one else in the office is an animator so I feel like that will put me in a good postion for getting more of this work if it comes in.
